  • 01

    Set-Theoretic Logic

    QCA views cases as configurations of set memberships rather than variables, focusing on relations of necessity and sufficiency.

  • 02

    Asymmetry

    The causes leading to the presence of an outcome may be entirely different from the causes leading to its absence.

  • 03

    Equifinality

    Multiple distinct combinations of conditions (different causal pathways) can lead to the exact same outcome.

  • 04

    Causal Complexity

    Conditions rarely work in isolation; they produce effects when combined as configurations (conjural causation).
